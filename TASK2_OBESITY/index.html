OBESITY AWARENESS

What is Obesity?

Obesity is a chronic complex disease defined by excessive fat deposits that can impair health. Obesity can lead to increased risk of type 2 diabetes and heart disease, it can affect bone health and reproduction, it increases the risk of certain cancers. Obesity influences the quality of living, such as sleeping or moving.

The diagnosis of overweight and obesity is made by measuring people’s weight and height and by calculating the body mass index (BMI): weight (kg)/height² (m²). The body mass index is a surrogate marker of fatness and additional measurements, such as the waist circumference, can help the diagnosis of obesity.

Causes of Obesity

Poor Diet

High-calorie intake from processed foods, sugary drinks, and junk food Lack of fruits, vegetables, and whole grains in the diet.

Physical Inactivity

Sedentary lifestyles with minimal physical activity.Reliance on vehicles, computers, and mobile devices.

Genetic Factors

Genes can affect how the body stores and distributes fat. Genetic predisposition may influence metabolism and appetite.

Hormonal Imbalances

Conditions like hypothyroidism, Cushing's syndrome, or polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS) can contribute to obesity.

Medications

Certain drugs like antidepressants, steroids, or antipsychotics may cause weight gain.

Psychological Factors

Emotional eating due to stress, anxiety, or depression
